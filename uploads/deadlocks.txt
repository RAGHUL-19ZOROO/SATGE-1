Deadlocks and synchronization:

- A critical section is the part of a program where shared resources are accessed.
- Synchronization tools include mutexes, semaphores, monitors, and hardware instructions.
- A deadlock is a situation where a set of processes waits forever for resources held by one another.

Deadlock conditions:
- Mutual exclusion
- Hold and wait
- No preemption
- Circular wait

Handling deadlocks:
- Prevention
- Avoidance
- Detection and recovery
